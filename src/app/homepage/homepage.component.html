“The love for all living creatures is the most noble attribute of a man” - Charles Darwin.

Dogs of Coimbatore (DoC), was born from this very innate love. Our non-profit was founded with a singular vision - to protect and care for all the animals on the streets.

There is a huge gap between the number of animals that need care and the number of organizations working for them in Coimbatore. We believe that taking care of them is not only the responsibility of the government but the onus lies on each one of us. We and the environment around us are a single unit, each depending on the other for their overall well-being. DoC is taking a small step in this direction.

Our preliminary focus is primarily on anti-rabies vaccinations, sterilization and public awareness. But as we gather momentum and the resources, we will expand to other areas that need attention. We have started working for the dogs and will eventually extend our efforts to other animals on the street as well. DoC has brought together experts in the field to be our backbone and guide us in the right direction.

We have big dreams, we hope you will be a part of the journey.

Our Focus

Anti-Rabies Vaccination

Rabies is a terrible disease that is fatal to both animals and humans. Prevention is the only way to tackle this. DoC will ensure that every street dog in Coimbatore is vaccinated.

Animal Birth Control

ABC is the only practical solution for controlling the dog population on the streets which in turn has numerous benefits such as eradicating rabies, preventing accidents and other causes of death, better health and well-being of dogs and more. DoC is backed by expert veterinarians to carry out the procedures and the after-surgery care of the dogs.

Awareness

No change is possible without the joint effort of the public. DoC aims to create awareness on various topics related to the welfare of animals and to mobilize the support of the public, especially the youth to ensure the sustainability of these practices.

About Us

Kesica is the founder of Dogs of Coimbatore. Dogs have been a huge part of her life since childhood. Her love for them led her to volunteer with a few NGOs in Coimbatore working for animal welfare. A few rescue calls that didn’t go well had a massive impact on her and she became acutely aware of the enormity of the problems. After deliberating with many people working in this area and fellow animal lovers, she decided to bridge the existing gap in animal welfare through DoC. There is a need for 24-hour hospitals, mobile clinics, systematic ABC surgeries, vaccinations, well organised and timely rescue services, steps against animal cruelty and a lot of awareness. She envisions doing it all one-by-one and making Coimbatore a model city for exemplary animal welfare services. She has teamed up with people who share a similar vision to make this enormous dream a reality.
